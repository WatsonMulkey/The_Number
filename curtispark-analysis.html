Overview
Executive Summary

Curtis Park Neighbors (CPN) operates a WordPress.com-hosted website serving as the digital hub for Denver's Curtis Park neighborhood — a Registered Neighborhood Organization (RNO) and Historic District. The site handles community communications, event coordination, historic preservation education, and donation collection.

This analysis identified several critical issues alongside a fundamentally sound content strategy. The site has rich, well-organized neighborhood information but is undermined by technical gaps that reduce discoverability, accessibility, and professional credibility.

Search Engine Optimization
SEO Analysis
CRITICAL: Meta Description Contains Theme Boilerplate
The homepage meta description currently reads: "Homepage content is primarily handled through the customize panel. Learn more here: https://wordpress.com/theme/prosperity"

This is default theme placeholder text, not a real description. Anyone finding CPN via Google sees WordPress setup instructions as the search snippet. This is the single highest-impact SEO fix available.

Missing Schema Markup

The site has zero JSON-LD structured data. For a neighborhood organization, the following schema types would enhance search presence:

  • Organization / NonprofitOrganization — name, address, logo, social profiles
  • Event — monthly meetings, community events
  • Place — Curtis Park neighborhood geographic data
  • BreadcrumbList — navigation path structure

WCAG Compliance
Accessibility Audit

For a community organization that may receive public funding or serve residents with disabilities, WCAG 2.1 AA compliance is both a best practice and a potential legal requirement. The site has some good foundations but significant gaps.

CRITICAL: 10 of 11 Images Missing Alt Text
91% of images on the homepage have no alt attribute or have empty alt text. This means screen reader users receive no context for visual content. Includes the hero image, section photos, and card thumbnails. Only the site logo has alt text.

Color Contrast Concerns

The site's primary action color is green (#10a510) used on buttons and links against the tan background (#f2dc99). This combination yields approximately a 3.2:1 contrast ratio, which passes WCAG AA for large text (18pt+) but fails for normal body text (requires 4.5:1). The green links within paragraph text are the primary concern.

Content Integrity
Content Issues Found
Duplicate Copy: "Maintaining Historic Homes" Card
The "Maintaining Historic Homes" card displays identical text to the "Accessory Dwelling Units" card: "ADUs continue the neighborhood's historic pattern of secondary structures..." — this is a copy/paste error. The description does not match the card topic.
Broken Link: "Resources for Historic Homes" Card
The card image links to /character-defining-features/ instead of /resources/. Users clicking the image are taken to the wrong page.
Non-Functional Link: "Maintaining Historic Homes"
The "Learn More" element on this card appears to be a styled <span> rather than a proper <a> anchor tag. It looks clickable but does not navigate anywhere.
Manual Events Management
The Events page uses hardcoded HTML list items rather than a calendar plugin or dynamic system. Events must be manually edited in the WordPress editor. There is no iCal feed, no recurring event support, and no calendar view. For an organization centered on monthly meetings, this creates ongoing maintenance burden and risk of outdated information.

Donation Flow Leaves the Site
Clicking "Make a Donation Online" redirects users entirely off curtispark.org to Square's hosted storefront. This creates friction, reduces trust for users unfamiliar with Square, and means CPN has no visibility into donation page analytics or abandonment rates.
Google Groups for Community Mailing Lists
The mailing lists use Google Groups with mailto: subscribe links. This approach provides no web-based signup form, no email templates, no open rate tracking, and no list management UI. Modern alternatives (Mailchimp, Buttondown, etc.) would provide significantly better engagement tools.

Contact Form Gaps

The contact form (Jetpack Forms) has only three fields: Name, Email, and Phone. There is no message body field — users can submit their contact information but cannot explain why they are reaching out. The form also lacks CAPTCHA protection, making it vulnerable to automated spam submissions.

Analytics Gap

The site uses Jetpack Stats only. No Google Analytics, no Google Search Console verification tag, and no third-party analytics were detected. Jetpack Stats provides basic pageview counts but lacks the depth needed for understanding user behavior, conversion tracking, or SEO performance monitoring.

Homepage Length & Content Weight
The homepage is extremely long-scrolling. The "Historic Home Alterations" section alone, with 6 feature cards plus the tax credits callout, consumes approximately 60% of the homepage vertical space. This section could be its own dedicated landing page, with a concise summary and link from the homepage.

Design & Visual Identity

Strengths

  • Consistent tan/green/brown palette evokes historic character
  • Typography (Merriweather + Source Sans Pro) is professional
  • Mobile responsive — collapses to hamburger menu

Weaknesses

Action Plan
Recommendations

Quick Wins — Low effort, high impact

1
Fix the meta description. Replace the WordPress theme boilerplate with a real description: e.g., "Curtis Park Neighbors is Denver's Curtis Park RNO and Historic District organization. Join us to preserve our neighborhood's history and plan for its future."
2
Add alt text to all images. 10 of 11 images are missing descriptive alt attributes. Provide context-appropriate descriptions for each.
3
Fix duplicate copy. The "Maintaining Historic Homes" card shows ADU content. Replace with appropriate description of home maintenance resources.
4
Fix broken link. "Resources for Historic Homes" image links to /character-defining-features/ instead of /resources/.
5
Remove empty heading tags. Delete or populate the 3 empty H2/H3 elements found on the homepage.
6
Add CAPTCHA to contact form. Enable Jetpack's built-in anti-spam or add reCAPTCHA to prevent automated submissions.

Medium Effort — Moderate effort, meaningful improvement

7
Add Schema.org structured data. Implement JSON-LD for NonprofitOrganization, Event, and BreadcrumbList to improve search presence and enable rich results.
8
Replace hardcoded events with a calendar solution. Use a WordPress events plugin or embed Google Calendar to reduce manual maintenance and provide iCal feeds.
9
Modernize mailing lists. Migrate from Google Groups mailto: links to a proper email marketing platform with web signup forms, analytics, and templated newsletters.
10
Add a message body field to the contact form. Users currently can only submit name/email/phone with no way to explain their inquiry.
11
Install Google Analytics and Search Console. Jetpack Stats alone does not provide the depth needed for understanding visitor behavior, search performance, or conversion tracking.
12
Convert images to WebP format. All current images are JPEG. WebP provides 25-35% smaller files with equivalent quality.

Strategic Investments — Higher effort, transformative impact

13
Platform migration. Move from WordPress.com managed hosting to self-hosted WordPress.org (or a modern static framework) for full plugin control, custom development, and removal of "Powered by WordPress.com" branding.
14
Homepage redesign. Reduce page length by moving the Historic Home Alterations deep content to its own landing page. Add clear visual hierarchy guiding users toward key actions (donate, attend meeting, join mailing list).
15
Integrate donation flow on-site. Replace the external Square redirect with an embedded payment form to reduce friction, maintain trust, and enable donation analytics.
16
Build an email newsletter system. Create branded newsletter templates with proper subscriber management, replacing the current Google Groups approach with a professional communications platform.
